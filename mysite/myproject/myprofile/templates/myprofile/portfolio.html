I am a seasoned educational leader and research professional with extensive experience in strategic planning and policy development in the education sector. I hold multiple master's degrees in Astronomy, Mathematical Science, and Applied Mathematics from prestigious institutions across the globe, including the University of Science and Technology of China and the University of Cape Town.

From 2018 to 2024, I served as the Deputy Minister for Planning, Research, and Development at the Ministry of Education in Liberia, where I led significant initiatives in education funding, policy evaluation, and strategic planning. My tenure was marked by successful engagements with international donors, raising over USD 300 million in direct support and supervising a portfolio of development projects worth more than USD 200 million. I spearheaded the development of Liberia’s new education sector plan, implemented innovative data collection tools, and led the national response to educational challenges during the COVID-19 pandemic.

Prior to this, I was the Executive Director at the Liberia Institute for Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics, revitalizing the institution and promoting STEM education through partnerships and innovative programs. My academic background is complemented by practical teaching experience in physics and mathematics at the University of Liberia and disaster risk analysis for Liberia’s education sector.

My technical expertise includes data modeling, data analysis, and proficiency in programming languages such as Python, JavaScript, and R. I am known for my strategic leadership, critical thinking, and ability to multitask effectively in high-stakes environments.
